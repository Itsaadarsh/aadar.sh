retain everything you read

22/07/2024

Original Article:
fs.blog/reading


  • ‣ One of the best parts of reading is that it allows you to master what other people have already figured out.
  • ‣ Reading the words is the easy part, but it is not enough if you want to retain and apply what you learn.
  • ‣ Not everything needs to be read the same way, tailoring “how you read” to “what you read” saves you time and increases retention.
  • ‣ When it comes to reading you don’t need to finish what you started you can quit.
  • ‣ Skim through a lot of books, and read a few. Immediately re-read the best ones twice.
  • ‣ Reading the same thing twice is better than reading 10 average ones.
  • ‣ Reading one great book slowly is better than quickly skimming through 100 average books.
  • ‣ A good book is like a good wine that deserves to be savored. Find something worth reading then chew on the ideas slowly and deeply.
  • ‣ You can’t get where you want to be without learning constantly, one of the best ways to learn is to read..
  • ‣ Reading habits don’t have to be complicated you can read just 25 pages every day..

“In my whole life, I have known no wise people who didn’t real all the time - none, zero.” (Charlie Munger)

"Read old books. Read the best ones twice.”
